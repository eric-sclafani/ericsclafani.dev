I liked this one, but for me, a lot of SK's descriptions were hard for me to visualize. The Shining absolutely does a great job instilling terror and claustrophobia in the reader, however. Most of the book is told through Danny's perspective

Most people know this title from Stanley Kubrick's adaptation. I personally think the book and film are different enough such that one is not better than the other. They are two different ways to tell the same tale. Stephen King's stories are famously character and narrative driven, which in my opinion, is difficult to adapt to the screen. Stanley Kubrick (who I'm just realizing shares the same initials as King) focuses less on the inner monologue the book has and more on creating visually beautiful but haunting scenes.
