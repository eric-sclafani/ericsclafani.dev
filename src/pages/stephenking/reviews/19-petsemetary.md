I really liked most of this book. Good characterization as usual, very detailed narrative. Where I think it fell short was the ending. SK is not known for writing great endings, and it felt like he wasn't sure how to end this novel. Nonetheless, still an amazing read.

This book is a perfect example of SK's tendency to not hold back with gruesome details. He's not afraid to make the reader squirm in their seats (or while standing on the subway, my usual reading environment).

The movie adaption was _awful_. Instead of pulling a Kubrick and changing the story to better suit the screen, the movie mostly makes very minor changes just for changes-sake. For example, there was no reason to change Jud Crandall's character into a creepy old man. It didn't add anything to the story. He was the _best_ character in the book by a longshot. Additionally, changing which kid dies also didn't affect the story at all. So why make these changes?
