This book felt more tame than many of SK's other books. To be honest, even though I still loved his detailed descriptions of the town and the environment, parts of Salem's Lot were on the boring side. I also wasn't a fan of Mark's character. He felt too smart as a kid, especially when almost all other characters become subjects of the vampires.

I get what SK was going for though because this comes up in It as well; children having a different response to evil that shouldn't exist because they haven't been corrupted by the world like adults have. But I think Mark's personality felt a little _too_ convenient for the plot.
