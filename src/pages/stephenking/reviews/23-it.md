Probably the best book I've read. Stephen King's attention to characterization is on full display here. Like many of his books, this one is more-so character and narrative driven rather than driven by plot. Since the book transitions between childhood and adulthood, I love how you can see their childhood traits reflected in their adult lives.

I tried watching the 2017 movie, but... I just couldn't. Within the first 20 minutes, I was already in pain with how much was changed. I'm sure the movie is great for the non-book readers, but because I hold SK's book with such high regard, I couldn't watch it. I also didn't think the child actors were that great, which only added to the pain for me. It made me just want to re-read the book again.
