To be honest, it's been a long while since I read Carrie at the time of this writing. So I may need to update it, but here goes.

As Stephen King's first novel, you can clearly see his acute attention to detail with the narrative and build-up to the climax. Additionally, SK has a tendency to feature kids and teenagers being subjected to...unfortunate fates.

The ending is the star of the show here though. I like how the narrative switches to Carrie's classmates so the reader can experience the carnage from several viewpoints. Also in SK fashion, the ending suggests that the telekinetic powers aren't unique to Carrie, ending on a cliff-hanger in that regard.
